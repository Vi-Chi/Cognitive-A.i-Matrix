Where Alita Fits
Battle Angel Alita / Gunnm is not just “cyborg girl fights.” It is one of the strongest examples of the Cyborg Self archetype.
Alita is rebuilt from discarded remains, has memory loss, discovers combat instincts, and has to reconcile “girl vs weapon,” “human vs machine,” and “individual vs soldier.” That makes her closer to Frankenstein + Pinocchio + Blade Runner compressed into one body. 
Her pattern:

Body is replaceable. Memory is broken. Violence is inherited. Identity must be chosen.

So Alita belongs beside:


Ghost in the Shell — body/mind boundary


Blade Runner — artificial humanity


Frankenstein — reconstructed life


Pinocchio — desire to become real


Akira — body as battlefield


Texhnolyze — cybernetics as social decay



Where Lain Fits
Serial Experiments Lain is not mainly an AI story. It is a networked identity / digital godhood story.
Lain is important because she bypasses the normal robot-body question. She is not asking “am I human even though I have machine parts?” She asks:

If identity can exist across the network, is the body still the self?

Recent scholarship contrasts Ghost in the Shell and Lain: Kusanagi is a physical cyborg confronting AI and memory, while Lain becomes a more abstract posthuman identity through the Wired. 
Her pattern:

Self becomes signal. Signal becomes system. System becomes god.
